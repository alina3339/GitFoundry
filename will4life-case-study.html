01  ·  The brief

Will4Life came to us with a quiet, serious ask: a website that would feel as careful as the conversations they have with clients. No sales pressure. No gimmicks. Just a single page that earned the visitor's trust in the first five seconds and made booking a confidential appointment the most obvious next step.

Their audience is people thinking about a subject most of us avoid. Anything loud or salesy would have been the wrong register. The site needed to feel like a firm handshake and a warm room — professional, measured, and gentle.

02  ·  Approach

Three deliberate decisions.

01

A sky-to-dusk gradient, not a photo.

Stock photography of hands and grandchildren would have felt generic. A soft blue-to-warm-amber gradient evokes continuity across generations without illustrating it literally — an emotional colour story that stays on brand on any screen.

02

Headline in italic serif, body in quiet sans.

"Clarity" is set in a warm italic Fraunces to feel human and considered, against a restrained Inter body. The pairing gives the page a legal-practice weight without ever tipping into corporate stiffness.

03

A confidential CTA, not a loud one.

"Book a Confidential Appointment" — exact copy the client uses on the phone. The button sits in the page's natural reading flow, not a pop-up or a sticky bar. The booking modal opens inline; nothing leaves the page.

04

A quote card, treated as a letter.

The right-hand card carries a poem written by the practice's founder. We set it in italic serif on a frosted-glass pane with hand-picked accent underlines — a literary moment on a page that could easily have defaulted to icons.

05

No tracking beyond what is necessary.

A single privacy-respecting analytics pixel. No Facebook Pixel, no third-party fonts that phone home, no heat-mapping. Visitors to a will-writer's site deserve more discretion than the industry default.

06

Hand-coded, hand-owned.

Static HTML on GitHub Pages. No WordPress to patch. No monthly renewal. The client holds the repository, the domain, and the full rights to the words and design. The site will outlive any platform.

04  ·  The finish

The full repository was handed over on day six. The client now owns every file — including the poem card, the gradient tokens, and the booking-modal logic. They can edit copy themselves, or ask us to. There is no subscription on the back of it and never will be.

Will4Life is exactly the kind of project GitFoundry was built for: careful people doing careful work, who deserve a website with the same posture. No platform, no lock-in, no monthly bill quietly eating into their margin. Just a page that shows up when someone needs them.
