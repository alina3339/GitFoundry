Discuss your project
A few questions, answered

The questions one tends to have, before deciding.

Is the hosting genuinely free, and is it really permanent?

The hosting is provided by GitHub Pages, a free service from GitHub (now part of Microsoft) which has been in continuous operation since 2008. The only ongoing cost the client incurs is the modest annual fee one pays to a domain registrar (somewhere around twelve pounds a year, paid to Namecheap, Porkbun, or whichever registrar one prefers). That money goes to the registrar directly; it does not pass through the studio.

And if GitHub were one day to change its terms?

Every site the studio delivers is portable by design. Included in the handover packet is a migration playbook which describes, step by step, how to redeploy the repository to Cloudflare Pages, Netlify, GitLab Pages, or any other host that serves static files. The procedure is rarely longer than an hour. The same source code, being plain HTML, works on all of them.

I am not technical. Can I really maintain the site myself?

Yes; the handover is, in fact, written for this case. The owner's manual is in plain English and refers to your particular pages by name. It is read aloud, against the live site, in a thirty minute walkthrough recording. Those who would rather not touch the repository at all are welcome to take a small retainer, or to commission the studio for occasional updates as life requires them.

How is this different from hiring a freelancer to do the same job?

The answer lies in what is delivered at the end of the work, rather than during it. Any competent developer can build a static page; comparatively few finish the job with a written manual, a runbook for unhappy moments, a migration playbook in case one ever wishes to leave, and a real support window. The studio's particular concern is that the site, once handed over, should remain operable without further dependence on the studio.

And if the studio itself were to cease trading?

The site keeps working. The repository already sits in your GitHub account; the domain is registered in your name; the hosting is on a platform that costs nothing and is operated by a company a great deal larger than this one. The continuity of the studio is not, by design, a condition of the continuity of your site.

Is the studio VAT registered?

Yes. All displayed prices include UK VAT; the invoice contains no figures one has not already seen.
